Examples of Searchs on BUENO

Some simple searches using default settings:

  • Map spotted bats from New Mexico.
    1. Go to the Specimen Search form ("Specimen" tab at top of page).
    2. Type "spotted bat " in Common Name, and "new mexico" in State/Province.
    3. From the dropdown at the bottom of the page, "Return result as," select "BerkeleyMapper Map."
    4. Click the search button and a wait a minute while BerkeleyMapper does its stuff.
  • Map brown bats for which there is DNA sequence.
    1. Go to the Specimen Search form ("Specimen" tab at top of page).
    2. Type "myotis" in Scientific Name.
    3. From the dropdown for "Other Identifier Type," select "GenBank sequence accession."
    4. Click the search button and get a list. Click on one of the catalog numbers to see the individual specimen record. From there, click on the GenBank sequence accession number to see the sequence in GenBank.pl

Some searches that use more than the default settings:

  • How many collared peccary specimens include frozen tissues?
    1. You must be logged in, having "your settings" as the right-most tab at the top of the page.
    2. On the Settings Form, click the green radio button for "Parts" then save your settings with the yellow button at the top and bottom of the form.
    3. Go to the Specimen Search form ("Specimen" tab at top of page) and this form should now include a "Parts" box.
    4. Select the Preservation Method of "frozen" from within the parts box.
    5. Type "collared peccary" in Common Name (near the top of the Search Form)
    6. Click on the search button; you should now have list of all collared peccaries with frozen parts, irrespective of which part is frozen.
  • Are there Myotis californicus that were collected between 10 October and the end of December?
    1. You must be logged in, having "your settings" as the right-most tab at the top of the page.
    2. On the Settings Form, click the green radio button for "Advanced Date Search" then save your preferences with the yellow button at the top and bottom of the form.
    3. Go to the Specimen Search form ("Specimen" tab at top of page) and this form should now include a six additional possiblities just below "Year Collected."
    4. In "Month Collected," select October (left dropdown) and December (right dropdown).
    5. In "Day Collected," select 10 (left dropdown) and 31 (right dropdown).
    6. Type "myotis californicus" in Scientific Name (near the top of the Search Form)
    7. Click on the search button; you should now have list of all Myotis californicus collected from 10 October to 31 December, irrespective of the year.
