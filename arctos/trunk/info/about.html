Catalog Data

"Catalog data" form the core of Arctos, and are roughly equivalent to the traditional data recorded with museum specimens.

Accessions record groups of specimens coming into the Museum, and include agents, a description, and estimated specimen count.

Collectors/Preparators: Arctos records any number of agents acting as Collectors or Preparators.

Identifications: Arctos records any number of identification determinations, only one of which may be accepted at any time. Identifications include any number of Agents, a data, and a method. Identifications are backed by formal taxonomy, but may consist of more than simply taxon names by way of formulae. Examples include:

Locations are split into several tables.
Higher Geography is a formalized string which varies in specificity from continent to county.
Locality adds an interpreted locality description and vertical dimension.
Coordinates are recorded as determinations, of which only one may be accepted at any time. Coordinates include extensive information about precision and determination method, and may be recorded in any format.
Collecting Event adds a verbatim locality description, method and source, and habitat information.

Other Identifiers: Any number of identifying "numbers" (consisting of strings, optionally split into prefix, integer number, and suffix) may be recorded with a specimen. If corresponding data are available, as in GenBank and BOLD, Other Identifiers may be structured to form external links.

Attributes record measurements and determination. Any number of attributes may be recorded, and all attributes include a method, a date, and a determiner. Attributes are collection-specific, and may use code-table controlled vocabularies. Examples include:

Parts are the actionable units of Arctos. They are loaned, barcoded, and tracked. Cataloged Items are essentially groups of parts. Parts may have a modifier, preservation method, disposition, condition, and may be scanned into Containers.

Usage

Arctos records specimen usage by way of Citations, which explicitly link Publications to Specimens.
